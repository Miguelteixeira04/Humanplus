THE TECHNOLOGICAL REVOLUTION

AI_HISTORY +
1950

THE TURING TEST

It all began with a simple question: "Can machines think?" In 1950, Alan Turing published his seminal paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing what is now known as the Turing Test [24].

1956

BIRTH OF AI

The field was officially born at the historic Dartmouth Summer Research Project. Organized by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon, this workshop coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" [25].

2012

DEEP LEARNING

After decades of "AI Winter," the landscape changed forever with AlexNet's victory in the ImageNet challenge [7]. This proved that neural networks driven by Big Data and GPUs could perceive the world.

2023+

GENERATIVE ERA

We have moved from systems that classify data to systems that create it. With the release of models like GPT-4 and Midjourney, AI demonstrated the ability to write code, compose poetry, and generate photorealistic imagery.

REFS: [06] Ponti et al. (2017) | [07] Krizhevsky et al. (2012) | [24] Turing (1950) | [25] McCarthy et al. (1956) | [29] DSA
DOC_02

DIGITAL MORAL FOUNDATIONS

ETHICS +

CLASSICAL THEORIES

As algorithms mediate reality, we must revisit ancient debates. Consequentialism vs Deontology [16]. The challenge is applying these human-centric frameworks to cold, autonomous code.

NEW DILEMMAS

Luciano Floridi, from Yale's Digital Ethics Center, argues that technology doesn't just exacerbate old problems but creates completely new moral dilemmas [1]. We are no longer just passive users; we are subjects of algorithmic governance.

Contemporary tech ethics must address four fundamental pillars: Responsibility, Transparency, Privacy, and Justice [3].

REFS: [01] Floridi (2024) | [03] Branford et al. (2024) | [16] BCU (2019)
DOC_03

ALGORITHMIC DECISION

ETHICS / BIAS +

THE BLACK BOX

A critical ethical issue identified by Vincent C. Müller is opacity. Many Deep Learning systems function as "Black Boxes"—we know the input and the output, but the internal logic remains a mystery [4]. This has led to the demand for Explainable AI (XAI) and the legal "Right to Explanation" [13][15].

REAL WORLD BIAS

Case Study: Amazon's Recruiting Tool: In 2015, Amazon scrapped an AI recruiting tool because it was biased against women. The system was trained on 10 years of resumes (mostly from men) and effectively "learned" that male candidates were preferable [9].

Case Study: Apple Card: In 2019, the Apple Card faced viral backlash due to gender discrimination in credit limits [11].

REFS: [09] Dastin (2018) | [13] UFSM (2024) | [17] Wright (2024)
DOC_04

FUTURE OF WORKFORCE

SOCIETY +

COGNITIVE DISRUPTION

Unlike the Industrial Revolution which automated muscle, the AI revolution targets cognitive and non-routine tasks. The World Economic Forum warns that millions of roles could be automated. Geoffrey Hinton recently stated that AI could shift human labor to a completely new paradigm [20].

THE CENTAUR MODEL

Despite fears of replacement, many researchers suggest the future is collaborative. In medicine and engineering, the best results often come from humans working with AI. As Floridi notes, how society manages this transition will determine whether the benefits of AI are shared equitably [1].

REFS: [01] Floridi (2024) | [20] Hinton (2025)
DOC_05

SYNTHETIC CREATIVITY

AI_CREATIVITY +

CREATIVITY REIMAGINED

For centuries, creativity was considered the exclusive domain of the human soul. However, generative models like ChatGPT, Midjourney, and Sora have shattered this perception. By recombining vast cultural references, algorithms can now produce art, code, and literature that feels new and relevant.

AUTHOR VS. CURATOR

Jason Branford argues that we shouldn't see this as simple copying, but as an intelligent reorganization of meaning [3]. The role of the human artist is shifting from "Creator" to "Curator" or "Director" of synthetic systems.

The Legal Battlefield: This shift brings complex legal wars. Timnit Gebru argues that we need new regulatory frameworks to protect human creators [22].

REFS: [03] Branford (2024) | [22] Gebru (2024)
DOC_06

GEOPOLITICS & POWER

POWER / RISK +

THE NEW ARMS RACE

Artificial Intelligence is the defining strategic resource of the 21st century. A triangular power dynamic has emerged: the US drives private innovation, China pursues a centralized state plan, and the EU acts as the global regulator [2].

AUTOMATED CONFLICT

The military application of AI poses existential risks. Systems that can select and engage targets without human intervention could lead to "flash wars". Geoffrey Hinton has warned that the uncontrolled evolution of military AI could lead to scenarios of automated conflict [21].

REFS: [02] Floridi (2018) | [21] Hinton (2024)

RISK_ASSESSMENT

TURING AWARD // PIONEER

YOSHUA BENGIO

“Efforts to build AI systems designed to act in their own interest should be criminalized.”

Yoshua Bengio, once optimistic, drastically changed his stance in 2023. He argues that humanity is not prepared for the speed of AI evolution. His primary concern is the loss of control: once an AI surpasses human intelligence, it may develop goals that conflict with our survival [19].

KEY PROPOSAL: International treaties to ban the development of autonomous agents capable of self-replication.
GODFATHER OF AI

GEOFFREY HINTON

“It is quite conceivable that humanity is just a passing phase in the evolution of intelligence.”

Hinton left Google to speak freely about the existential risks. He suggests that digital intelligence is structurally superior to biological intelligence due to the speed of learning. He warns that AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) could arrive within the next decade [20][21].

KEY PROPOSAL: Immediate global pause on giant experiments and regulation of military AI.
ETHICS SPECIALIST

TIMNIT GEBRU

“The danger is not that AI will destroy us, but that it will be used to control us.”

Unlike Hinton and Bengio, Gebru focuses on immediate harms. Her seminal paper "Stochastic Parrots" argues that Large Language Models mimic human speech without understanding, amplifying racism, sexism, and centralization of power [22][23].

KEY PROPOSAL: Focus on labor rights, stopping data exploitation, and preventing algorithmic bias.
